My Story

Where It Started

I grew up in Tehran, Iran, a diverse, vibrant, and endlessly creative city. Being surrounded by people from different backgrounds taught me empathy, persistence, and the importance of shared understanding.

These experiences shaped my approach to teamwork, problem-solving, and communication — skills that continue to guide me academically and professionally.
The Path I’m On

At York University’s Lassonde School of Engineering, I discovered how engineering blends creativity and precision. I was drawn to software because it gives me the freedom to build, experiment, and turn ideas into something real.

During my Co-op at RBC and while developing Tomorrow’s Trust, I gained a deeper understanding of both technical and entrepreneurial environments — a combination that continues to push my thinking forward.

What Inspires Me

One of my biggest inspirations was my high school computer science teacher. He believed that creativity is just as important as logical precision. That perspective helped shape how I view engineering — as a form of storytelling.

I believe the best engineers are people who understand human needs deeply and design solutions that improve lives, even in small ways.

“Build what you wish existed.”
